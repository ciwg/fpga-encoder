## Open Collaboration Loop (Promise-Theory-Aligned Workflow)

**Purpose:**
Enable coordination and collaboration **without hierarchy**, using visibility and voluntary engagement instead of delegation.

---

### Core Idea

Instead of asking people directly:

> Make your work **visible, legible, and “pull-able”**, so others can choose to engage.

---

### Structure

#### **A. Ongoing Work Log (public + lightweight)**

Keep a running log (doc, repo, or thread):

* What I’m working on
* Current state
* Where I’m stuck
* Next intended step

Keep entries short and frequent.

---

#### **B. “Help Signals” (explicit prompts)**

When blocked or unsure, clearly mark:

* **[Help Wanted]**
* **[Feedback Needed]**
* **[Pairing Opportunity]**

Include:

* what kind of help is needed
* estimated scope (quick answer vs deeper session)

---

#### **C. Invitation Model (not assignment)**

Instead of asking specific people:

* “If you see something here you can help with, reach out”
* “Happy to pair if anyone’s interested in this part”

This aligns with:

* voluntary participation
* incentive (hourly work)
* autonomy

---

#### **D. Collaboration Flow (when someone engages)**

1. Quick sync (async or short call)
2. Clarify problem
3. Decide:

   * pair
   * split work
   * handoff piece
4. Return outcomes to shared log

---

### Deliverable Output

A single artifact (doc or repo section) that includes:

* your live work log
* clearly marked help signals
* short notes from collaborations
* evolving decisions

---

### Success Signal

* Teammates **proactively engage** without being assigned
* You are **not blocked alone for long**
* Work naturally distributes based on interest and availability

---

### Constraint

Keep it:

* visible
* lightweight
* consistent

Not:

* over-structured
* meeting-heavy
* dependent on permission

---

### Framing

Your role here is:

> “Create conditions where collaboration happens naturally, not by authority.”

This directly tests the kind of decentralized coordination model you’re aiming to build.
